ORBITA | 20-01-2025

My code can be viewed on my GitHub, and is only playable using Python. I am thinking about remaking it using JavaScript so it can be played online.

¤ Part 1: What is Orbita

Orbita is a simple asteroid shooter I made to practice object-oriented programming. The objective is to shoot asteroids and collect a resource called Astroiris. You can also shoot a charged bullet that gravitates the asteroids, combining them into one larger asteroid.

If I remake this project, I would improve the particle system and create a belt object of asteroids that handles updates and collisions.

¤ Part 2: Music and Sound

The soundtrack for the game can be found on Spotify. I made it with my friend Konrad (LindebergProd) primarily in one sitting, and I am very proud of our work.

The spaceship also makes whooshing sounds that scale with speed and movement, which I feel is a very nice touch. I would love to improve the sound design of the normal rocket fire. I cannot stress enough how important sound design is for basic and repetitive sounds.

¤ Part 3: Jittery White Dots

I spent a lot of time working on giving the asteroids, spaceship, and bullets life by adding particles with a similar color. This gave life to a somewhat empty game. I will take this with me in my later projects, but I will make my particles more deliberate and physics-based, with gravity, weight, and so on.

¤ Part 4: Asteroids

I chose to make asteroids based on real images that I scaled down and drew. I like to imagine that this must be greatly appreciated by the astronomy community. I should have made a glossary with the names of the asteroids, how the pictures were taken and who took them.

¤ Part 5: You Died, But Your Death Inspired Others

When you die, you receive a motivational messages. This is inspired by Undertale, but is based on quotes that I find appealing:

> "You lose, yet try again...
> that's why others depend on you."

> "It will take you long...
> but the time will pass anyway."



¤ Part 6: Someone Answered Your Call

Something my game lacks is story and dynamic gameplay. I would like to add waves of asteroids, giving you a feeling of accomplishment after defeating each wave, and a captain who has a motive that explains why we are destoying the asteroids in the first place.
